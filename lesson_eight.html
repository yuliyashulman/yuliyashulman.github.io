The last lesson of the series. I was tempted to tech the girls about functions and data structures, but decided to end on a high note, and give them a safe space to experiment. By this point, most of the girls have been exploring on their own, but they were not equaly comfortable at doing so.

Lesson plan

As it became our custom, the lesson began with the demonstration of my student's independent work. At this point most girls prepared a project to showcase.

Then we moved on to the material I wanted to cover in class. This project's idea I borrowed from google. I wanted to have something unified enough to be a group activity, but different enough, to facilitate independent thinking. Animating the letters of the name seems like a great find. We each ended up with different letters, and different tricks, but we did share the ideas and looked at each other's code. Here is mine.

Actual lesson

Actual lesson went as planned. It undescored how much the girls have learned as programmers, and how much I've learned as a teacher. It took us a lot longer than 8 weeks, because of the travel plans, and family circumstances, but we did get here. By now school has already started, and we need to take a break, which will also give me a chance to think of a new project. The girls are all eager to continue. I want to move away from scratch, but have to dedicate some research to unifying their development environment.
